    Dancehall musician and rapper known for collaborating with fellow hip hop artist KRS-One. He released his debut album, Real Ting, in 1994. He contributed to Salt-N-Pepa's 1997 album Brand New. In 1997, he topped the US reggae chart with "Carpenter."

    Before Fame

    After moving to New York, he met reggae performer Super Cat at Super Power Records. At Super Cat's suggestion, he adopted his professional name, an acronym for Musical Assassin Delivering Lyrical Intelligence Over Nations. 

    Trivia

    He appeared in the music video for the Flava in Ya Ear remix by Craig Mack featuring Notorious B.I.G., Rampage, LL Cool J and Busta Rhymes. His awards include the 1994 Source Award for Reggae Artist of the Year and the 1995 Source Award for Reggae Hip-Hop Artist of the Year.

    Family Life

    He has Jamaican heritage and was born in London.

    Associated With

     He added hip hop rhythms to Shabba Ranks' "Jam."
